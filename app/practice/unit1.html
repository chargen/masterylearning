Propositional Logic

Atomic Propositions

Atomic propositions are constants A, B, C, ..., which can have either the value true or false.

Logical Connectices

Besides atomic propositions, we have the logical connectives `not`(not), `^^`(and), `vv`(or), `->`(implies).

Well-formed propositional formulas

Then a well-formed propositional formula `f` is:
  • `true`
  • `false`
  • `not g`, where g is a well-formed propositional formula
  • `g ^^ h`, where g and h are well-formed propositional formulas
  • `g vv h`, where g and h are well-formed propositional formulas
  • `g -> h`, where g and h are well-formed propositional formulas
  • `(g)`, where g is a well-formed propositional formula

Literals

A literal is either an atom or a negated atom.

Examples

  • `A ^^ B`
  • `A vv B`
  • `(A ^^B) vv (C ^^ D)`
